# The Failure Oracle — Demo Script v2 (Storyboard)
**One story, end to end · ~2:55 at natural pace · record voiceover at 1.0× — no fast-forward**

> **The story in one line:** Every startup that failed had the warning in its own numbers — nobody could read it in time. The Oracle reads it in seconds, argues both sides, shows the way out, and gets smarter every time it's used.

---

## Golden rules (read before recording)
- **Record the voiceover separately, at natural pace (1.0×).** The audio is the master track. The screen recording is silent and gets trimmed to match the words — never speed up the voice.
- **Hold the three money shots — don't rush these:** the **Oracle Score hitting 0 / CRITICAL**, the **DISPUTED** badge, and the **Cascade graph**. Let each breathe for a beat.
- **Everything else can be fast** — tighten the terminal and the scale-sweep montage freely (speeding *silent* screen footage is fine; there's no voice on it).
- Dark mode on · browser zoom 90% (110% for the Oracle Score shot only).

## Pre-flight setup (do before you hit record)
- App open on the **Landing hero**, WeWork **not yet run** (we run it live).
- **Clear WeWork's run history** (Monthly Tracking → Clear) so there's no "previous context."
- **Pre-run the Decision Auditor + Pre-Mortem** (Act 8): on the Decision Auditor tab click the **"Hire before PMF"** example → **Audit This Decision** → then **"Run Pre-Mortem →"** and let it finish, so both results are on screen to scroll through.
- **Slack channel open** with a pre-existing alert visible (don't trigger live — just cut to it).
- **"How it Works → Tech"** panel reachable in one click.

# WHY JUDGES WILL LOVE IT

**It's a story, not a feature tour.** One spine — *the warning was in the numbers, nobody could read it in time* — opens it (Act 1), pays off at the warning signals (*"five months, unused,"* Act 8), and resolves at the close. Every beat hands off to the next, so it flows.

**Every claim is concrete and verifiable** — 54× vs 4×, Δ35pp, 3% survival, 150 days — which reads as *real*, not "AI-powered" hand-waving.

**It maps cleanly to all three judging axes:**

| Judging axis | Where it shows up | 
|---|---|
| **Google Cloud — ADK, Gemini, Vertex AI** | 3-agent pipeline (Act 3), live terminal, engine callout (Act 16), Cloud Run + Cloud Scheduler (Acts 13, 16) |
| **MongoDB — breadth + depth, in the critical path** | Vector Search + BM25/RRF (Act 3), `$graphLookup` cascade (Act 10), Change Streams self-learning (Acts 10, 15), `$bucket`/`$facet` cohort (Act 14), ACID + MCP (engine, Act 16) |
| **Quality of idea** | The adversarial **DISPUTE** (Act 6), deterministic **escape-plan algebra** (Act 9), the **self-improving cascade** (Act 10) — three things most demos can't show |

**The single differentiator to land hard:** Act 6 — a second agent that *disagrees with a specific 35-point gap*. Most demos show an AI being confidently right; yours shows it cross-examining itself. That's the moment a judge remembers.

## Timing math (so you stay under 3:00)
- ~440 words of narration ≈ **~3:05–3:10 at a natural pace.** The six per-tab scale-sweep sentences (Act 14) add ~15s over a bundled montage — a deliberate choice to name every tool in a full sentence.
- If your limit is firm at 3:00, an **invisible ~1.03× nudge** on the final timeline pulls it to ~3:00 — undetectable to a listener (that's *only* the picture+audio together at 1.03×, which at that tiny ratio doesn't sound sped-up).
- The verdict (Act 4) stays deliberately punchy — that's the climax; don't pad it.
- Quickest places to claw back a few seconds if you ever need them: drop "On paper, still a darling" (Act 2 · ~2s), shorten Act 8's tail to "five months, unused" (~2s), or fold Act 15 into Act 16 — which also removes the awkward back-to-Cascade jump (~3s).
