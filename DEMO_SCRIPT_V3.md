# The Failure Oracle — Demo Script v3 (Storyteller cut)
**One story, told simply · ~3:05 at a natural pace · record the voiceover at 1.0× — never speed up the voice**

> **The story in one line:** Every startup that died left a warning in its own numbers. Nobody could read it in time. The Oracle reads it in seconds — argues both sides, shows the way out, and gets smarter every time.

> **What changed from v2:** same beats, same screens — but the *spoken* lines are now short and plain. Say it simple, show it precise: the jargon and exact figures stay **on screen**, the voice stays human. A judge feels the story and sees the rigor.

---

## Golden rules (read before recording)
- **Record the voiceover separately, at a natural storyteller pace.** Slow down. Let the short lines land. The audio is the master track; the silent screen recording is trimmed to match the words.
- **Hold the three money shots — don't rush them:** the **Oracle Score hitting 0 / CRITICAL**, the **DISPUTED** badge, and the **Cascade graph**. Let each breathe.
- **Pause on the periods.** This script is written in short lines on purpose. Every period is a small beat. Don't run them together.
- Dark mode on · browser zoom 90% (110% for the Oracle Score shot only).

## Pre-flight setup (do before you hit record)
- App open on the **Landing hero**, WeWork **not yet run** (we run it live).
- **Clear WeWork's run history** (Monthly Tracking → Clear) so there's no "previous context."
- **Pre-run the Decision Auditor + Pre-Mortem** (Act 14): Decision Auditor tab → **"Hire before PMF"** example → **Audit This Decision** → **"Run Pre-Mortem →"** and let it finish, so both are on screen to scroll.
- **Slack channel open** with a pre-existing alert visible (don't trigger live — just cut to it).
- **"How it Works → Tech"** panel reachable in one click.

# WHY JUDGES WILL LOVE IT

**It's a story, not a feature tour.** One spine — *the warning was in the numbers, nobody could read it in time* — opens it (Act 1), pays off at the warning signals (*"five months, unused"* — Act 8), and resolves at the close. Every beat hands to the next.

**Simple to hear, rigorous to see.** The voice stays plain so anyone follows it; the exact figures — 54× vs 4×, 3% survival, 150 days — live on screen where they read as *real*, not "AI-powered" hand-waving.

**It maps cleanly to all three judging axes:**

| Judging axis | Where it shows up |
|---|---|
| **Google Cloud — ADK, Gemini, Vertex AI** | 3-agent pipeline (Act 3), live terminal, engine callout (Act 15), Cloud Run + Cloud Scheduler (Acts 13, 15) |
| **MongoDB — breadth + depth, in the critical path** | Vector + keyword search (Act 3), graph cascade (Act 10), self-learning change streams (Acts 10, 15), cohort analytics (Act 14), the full Atlas stack (engine, Act 15) |
| **Quality of idea** | The agent that **disagrees** (Act 6), the deterministic **escape plan** (Act 9), the **self-improving cascade** (Act 10) — three things most demos can't show |

**The moment a judge remembers:** Act 6 — a second agent that *pushes back on the first*. Most demos show an AI being confidently right. Yours shows it cross-examining itself.

## Timing math (so you stay near 3:00)
- ~400 words of narration ≈ **~3:00–3:10 at a natural storyteller pace** (~135–140 wpm with the held beats).
- The voice is the master. If you ever need to trim to a hard 3:00, an invisible ~1.03× nudge on the *final* combined timeline is undetectable.
- The verdict (Act 4) stays short on purpose — it's the climax. Don't pad it; let the silence do the work.
- Quickest seconds to claw back if needed: drop "The world still loved it" (Act 2, ~2s), shorten Act 8's tail to just "Five months. Unused." (~2s), or trim one montage line in Act 14 (~2s).
